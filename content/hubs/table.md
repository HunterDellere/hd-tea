<p>Everything in the atlas ends at the table. These pages are the practical layer: how to brew each type and why the parameters are what they are, what a gaiwan changes that a mug cannot, and the words a taster actually needs (fewer than the tasting-wheel posters suggest). Steep parameters across the site are written in one grammar: temperature, leaf-to-water ratio, vessel, then times per infusion, so anything you read here can be carried straight to the kettle.</p>
