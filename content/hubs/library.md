<p>Chinese tea classification already did the design work: the six types are named by the color they pour. Green, white, yellow, oolong, black, dark. The circle beside each entry below is that tea's actual liquor at standard parameters, so a shelf of entries reads the way a tasting flight does. Tisanes sit on their own shelf at the end: chamomile, hibiscus, rooibos and their kin are not Camellia sinensis, and the atlas never pretends otherwise, but they are brewed and read with the same care.</p>
